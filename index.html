Itinerary Overview

Chasing Cherry Blossoms

13 Days exploring the historical streets of Seoul and the natural volcanic wonders of Jeju Island during peak spring bloom.

Dates

26 Mar - 07 Apr

Destinations

Seoul & Jeju

To-Do List

Don't forget to book your domestic flights from Seoul Gimpo (GMP) to Jeju (CJU) for Mar 29, and the return for Apr 2. Also, secure a rental car in Jeju in advance!

Seoul Part 1

Settle in and explore the historical heart of South Korea.

1
Fri, 26 Mar

Arrival & Night Market

  • 3:40 PM: Arrive at Incheon Airport (ICN). Pick up a T-Money transit card at a CU or GS25 convenience store (grab a classic Banana Milk while you're at it!), then catch the AREX Express Train directly to the city center.
  • 5:30 PM: Check into your hotel. Myeongdong or Hongdae are perfect base camps for first-time visitors with easy transit access. Take a moment to freshen up.
  • 7:00 PM: Welcome to Korea! Head out to the Myeongdong Night Market. Graze on street food like hotteok (sweet pancakes), tteokbokki, grilled cheese lobster, Tanghulu (candied fruit), and the viral 10 Won bread.
  • 9:00 PM: Stroll around the vibrant neon-lit streets, pop into an Olive Young flagship store to check out K-beauty trends, and snap your first trip photos at a Life4Cuts photo booth.
2
Sat, 27 Mar

Palaces & Traditions

  • 9:30 AM: Explore Gyeongbokgung Palace. Pro-tip: Rent a Hanbok from a nearby shop—it gets you free palace admission and makes for incredible souvenir photos. Catch the Royal Guard Changing Ceremony at 10 AM.
  • 12:30 PM: Lunch at Tosokchon Samgyetang, arguably the most famous ginseng chicken soup restaurant in Seoul, located in a traditional hanok just steps from the palace.
  • 2:00 PM: Wander the charming, hilly alleys of Bukchon Hanok Village. Stop by a traditional teahouse like Cha-teul for views over the tiled roofs.
  • 4:30 PM: Stroll down to Insadong to browse local artisan crafts, galleries, and the spiral Ssamziegil mall. Then, take a relaxing dusk walk along the restored Cheonggyecheon Stream.
  • 7:00 PM: Dinner at the historic Gwangjang Market. Grab a seat at a lively stall and order Bindaetteok (crispy mung bean pancakes), Mayak Gimbap, and homemade dumplings (Netflix famous!).
3
Sun, 28 Mar

Skyline Views & Culture

  • 10:00 AM: Take the scenic cable car up to N Seoul Tower. Enjoy panoramic views of the sprawling city and check out the famous "Love Locks" terrace. Keep an eye out for early spring blooms in Namsan Park.
  • 1:00 PM: Head down to the Hannam-dong / Itaewon area. Grab lunch, then visit the stunning Leeum Museum of Art, featuring both traditional Korean art and modern contemporary pieces.
  • 3:30 PM: Visit the spectacular National Museum of Korea nearby. The architecture is stunning, the grounds are beautiful, and it offers a deep dive into Korea's rich history.
  • 7:00 PM: Dinner time! Treat yourself to a premium Korean BBQ experience in Itaewon (like Maple Tree House) to enjoy high-quality pork belly (Samgyeopsal) roasted right at your table.

Jeju Island

Fly south for volcanic landscapes, coastal drives, and peak Jeju Cherry Blossoms.

4
Mon, 29 Mar

Hello, Jeju!

  • Morning: Take a short domestic flight from Seoul Gimpo (GMP) to Jeju (CJU). Pick up your rental car at the airport to unlock the island's best hidden gems.
  • 11:30 AM: Drive through Jeonnong-ro Cherry Blossom Street. Late March is absolute peak bloom in Jeju, and the street forms a breathtaking pink canopy.
  • 1:00 PM: Grab a late lunch of fresh Seafood Ramyeon at Nolman or a similar seaside spot in Aewol. Afterwards, walk the Aewol Handam Coastal Trail. The crystal clear water against the black volcanic rock is gorgeous.
  • 4:00 PM: Stop at a famous seaside cafe (like Cafe Knotted or Bomnal Cafe) for a sweet treat and sunset views.
  • 7:30 PM: Explore the Dongmun Night Market in Jeju City. It's bustling, loud, and the perfect place to try Jeju tangerine juice and abalone gimbap.
5
Tue, 30 Mar

West & South Wonders

  • 9:30 AM: Visit the Osulloc Tea Museum. Wander the expansive green tea fields, try a matcha roll cake, and visit the adjacent Innisfree Jeju House to make your own volcanic clay soap.
  • 11:30 AM: Stop by Hyeopjae Beach to admire the emerald waters and brilliant white sand, with perfect views of Biyangdo Island in the distance.
  • 2:00 PM: Drive by the dramatic Sanbangsan Mountain, which in springtime is famously surrounded by oceans of bright yellow canola flowers.
  • 4:30 PM: Head south to Seogwipo to view the spectacular Cheonjiyeon Waterfall, accessible via a beautifully landscaped walking path.
  • 7:00 PM: Dinner in Seogwipo. Try Jeju's famous Galchi Jorim (spicy braised cutlassfish)—a local seafood delicacy.
6
Wed, 31 Mar

Sunrise Peak & East Coast

  • 9:00 AM: Hike Seongsan Ilchulbong (Sunrise Peak). This UNESCO World Heritage site offers a short, steep hike culminating in incredible views of a green volcanic crater and the ocean.
  • 11:30 AM: Descend to the nearby cove to watch the famous Haenyeo (Jeju's legendary female free-divers) demonstrate their impressive catch and unique breathing techniques.
  • 2:00 PM: Walk the stunning Seopjikoji coastal trails. This scenic cape is renowned for its romantic lighthouse and rolling hills, heavily featured in Korean dramas.
  • 4:00 PM: Explore the Manjanggul Lava Tube, one of the finest lava tunnels in the world with fascinating rock formations. (Bring a light jacket as it's chilly inside!).
  • 5:30 PM: Stop at a local tangerine farm cafe (like Cafe the Container) for fresh hallabong ade.
7
Thu, 01 Apr

Udo Island Getaway

  • 9:00 AM: Take the short 15-minute passenger ferry from Seongsan Port across to the miniature paradise of Udo Island.
  • 10:30 AM: Rent electric bikes or a small electric sidecar to circumnavigate the island. The coastal roads are completely flat and beautiful.
  • 1:00 PM: Stop at Hagosu-dong Beach. Eat at a seaside cafe like Blanc Rocher and make sure to try the island's famous specialty: Udo Peanut Ice Cream!
  • 4:00 PM: Catch the ferry back to Jeju. On your drive back towards the city, make a sunset stop at Hamdeok Beach.
  • 7:00 PM: Treat yourselves to an authentic, sizzling Jeju Black Pork BBQ dinner—the meat is famously thick, juicy, and cooked over charcoal.

Seoul Part 2

Return to the capital just in time for the spectacular cherry blossom peak.

8
Fri, 02 Apr

Return to the Capital

  • 9:00 AM: Final morning in Jeju. Visit Dongmun Traditional Market to buy edible souvenirs like Hallabong orange chocolates and Omegi-tteok (rice cakes).
  • Midday: Drop off your rental car and fly from Jeju (CJU) back to Seoul Gimpo (GMP).
  • 4:00 PM: Check into your Seoul hotel. Head to Gangnam to visit the Starfield COEX Mall and snap a photo in front of the colossal, floor-to-ceiling Starfield Library.
  • 5:30 PM: Walk across the street to Bongeunsa Temple, a serene Buddhist temple providing a stark, beautiful contrast to the towering skyscrapers surrounding it.
  • 8:00 PM: Head to Banpo Hangang Park to watch the Banpo Bridge Rainbow Fountain show illuminate the river.
9
Sat, 03 Apr

Cherry Blossom Festival

  • 10:00 AM: Rent bikes and cycle through Yeouido Hangang Park. Walk the famous cherry blossom tunnels lined with over 1,800 blossoming Yoshino cherry trees! This is the epicenter of Seoul's spring bloom.
  • 12:30 PM: Have a picnic by the Han River. Do as the locals do and order 'Chimaek' (Korean fried chicken and beer) or instant ramen for delivery right to your picnic mat.
  • 3:00 PM: Visit The Hyundai Seoul, right across the street. It's one of the largest and most visually stunning department stores in the country. Grab a coffee at Camel Coffee inside.
  • 6:30 PM: Explore the vibrant university district of Hongdae for youthful energy, street fashion, and incredible indie buskers. Finish the night by renting a private Noraebang (karaoke) room!
10
Sun, 04 Apr

Lake Blooms & City Views

  • 10:00 AM: Walk the loop at Seokchon Lake Park. The entire lake is surrounded by a dense ring of blooming cherry trees, offering beautiful framing of Lotte World's Disney-esque Magic Island in the center.
  • 12:00 PM: Option to visit Lotte World Theme Park, or explore the massive Lotte World Mall (including the impressive indoor aquarium).
  • 3:00 PM: Head up to the Seoul Sky Observatory inside the Lotte World Tower (the 6th tallest building in the world). Brave the glass-bottomed observation deck!
  • 5:30 PM: Take a short taxi or train to Olympic Park for a relaxing dusk walk and to photograph the famous "Lone Tree" against the sunset.
11
Mon, 05 Apr

Trendy Cafes & Design

  • 10:00 AM: Rent a bike or stroll through Seoul Forest Park. Beyond cherry blossoms, you'll find vibrant tulip gardens, botanical houses, and a deer enclosure.
  • 1:00 PM: Explore Seongsu-dong (often called the "Brooklyn of Seoul"). Check out the flagship Dior concept store and industrial warehouses converted into massive, highly-aesthetic cafes (make sure to visit Cafe Onion, Daelim Changgo, or Nudake).
  • 5:00 PM: Visit the futuristic Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP), designed by Zaha Hadid. The architecture is mesmerizing at golden hour.
  • 7:30 PM: Have dinner in Dongdaemun, then dive into the massive wholesale fashion night markets that famously stay open until 5 AM.
12
Tue, 06 Apr

Last Minute Shopping & Spa

  • Morning: Enjoy a free morning. Alternatively, book a half-day tour to the DMZ (Demilitarized Zone) to view the North Korean border and the 3rd Infiltration Tunnel.
  • 1:00 PM: Relax at a traditional Korean bathhouse (Jjimjilbang) like Aquafield or Spa Lei. Soak in the hot pools and try a traditional Korean body scrub to soothe your muscles after days of walking.
  • 3:30 PM: Final shopping run! Stock up on skincare masks in Myeongdong, or hunt for bargains, souvenirs, and try Kalguksu (knife-cut noodles) at Namdaemun Market. Don't forget to check out the Myeongdong Underground Shopping Center for affordable fashion!
  • 7:30 PM: Farewell Dinner! End the trip on a high note by treating yourselves to premium, melt-in-your-mouth Korean Hanwoo Beef BBQ—the wagyu of Korea.
13
Wed, 07 Apr

Departure

  • 7:00 AM: Grab a quick, iconic Korean breakfast sandwich from Isaac Toast or Egg Drop before checking out.
  • 7:30 AM: Take the AREX express train back to Incheon International Airport (ICN). Get there early to enjoy the incredible airport facilities (they have robot cafes and indoor gardens!).
  • 11:05 AM: Flight SQ 611 departs. Have a safe journey back to Perth!
