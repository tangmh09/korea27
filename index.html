Itinerary Overview

Chasing Cherry Blossoms

13 Days exploring the historical streets of Seoul and the natural volcanic wonders of Jeju Island during peak spring bloom.

Dates

26 Mar - 07 Apr

Destinations

Seoul & Jeju

To-Do List

Don't forget to book your domestic flights from Seoul Gimpo (GMP) to Jeju (CJU) for Mar 31, and the return for Apr 3. Also, secure a rental car in Jeju in advance!

Seoul Part 1

Settle in and explore the vibrant neighborhoods of South Korea's capital.

1
Fri, 26 Mar

Arrival & Welcome

  • 3:40 PM: Arrive at Incheon Airport (ICN). Head to a GS25 or CU convenience store in the terminal to buy and top-up a T-Money transit card, and grab a famous Korean Banana Milk for the train ride!
  • 5:00 PM: Take the AREX Express Train directly to Seoul Station (43 mins), then transfer to your hotel and check in.
  • 7:00 PM: Welcome to Korea! Keep it easy tonight. Walk to a nearby bustling area to grab your first taste of authentic Korean BBQ.
  • 8:30 PM: Shake off the jet lag with a peaceful dusk walk along the Cheonggyecheon Stream, beautifully illuminated at night.
2
Sat, 27 Mar

Namdaemun & Gwangjang

  • 10:00 AM: Dive into Namdaemun Market, Korea's oldest traditional market. Hunt for souvenirs, camera gear, and try the famous vegetable hotteok (fried pancake).
  • 12:30 PM: Eat lunch in Namdaemun's legendary Kalguksu Alley. You'll get massive, handmade bowls of knife-cut noodles and spicy cold noodles for just a few dollars.
  • 2:30 PM: Walk over to view the majestic Sungnyemun Gate, then hop the subway to Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP) to admire the futuristic, spaceship-like architecture designed by Zaha Hadid.
  • 6:00 PM: Head to the historic Gwangjang Market for dinner. Fight for a seat at a lively stall and order Bindaetteok (crispy mung bean pancakes), Mayak Gimbap, and homemade dumplings (Netflix famous!).
3
Sun, 28 Mar

Seongsu Area & The River

  • 10:00 AM: Grab a morning coffee and stroll through Seoul Forest Park. Feed the deer, check out the botanical greenhouse, and enjoy the early spring tulips.
  • 12:30 PM: Explore Seongsu-dong (the "Brooklyn of Seoul"). Check out the spectacular flagship Dior concept store. Eat lunch and cafe-hop through industrial warehouses converted into massive aesthetic bakeries (like Cafe Onion or Daelim Changgo).
  • 4:00 PM: Rent a bike or a swan pedal-boat at the nearby Ttukseom Hangang Park right on the edge of the Han River.
  • 7:00 PM: Cross the university district to Kondae Taste Street for an energetic, youthful nightlife vibe. Enjoy some Jokbal (braised pig's trotters) or spicy stir-fry for dinner.
4
Mon, 29 Mar

Gangnam & Insadong

  • 10:00 AM: Head south of the river to Gangnam. Visit the underground Starfield COEX Mall and snap photos of the colossal, towering bookshelves at the Starfield Library.
  • 12:30 PM: Step outside the mall into the serene Bongeunsa Temple, a beautiful contrast of ancient Buddhist architecture against Gangnam's modern skyscrapers.
  • 3:00 PM: Take the subway back north to Insadong. Browse the antique shops, local artisan crafts, and the spiral Ssamziegil shopping complex. Stop for traditional tea and sweets.
  • 6:30 PM: Walk to the adjacent Ikseon-dong Hanok Village. This is one of Seoul's trendiest spots—a maze of 1920s traditional houses transformed into beautifully designed restaurants, pubs, and cafes. Perfect for dinner!
5
Tue, 30 Mar

Myeongdong Area

  • 10:30 AM: Spend the morning exploring the massive Shinsegae or Lotte Duty-Free Department stores for high-end goods, and make sure to eat lunch in their incredible basement food halls.
  • 1:30 PM: Dive deep into Myeongdong. Visit the Olive Young flagship store to stock up on K-beauty. Consider booking ahead for a Korean skin-clinic facial or a famous 15-step Korean scalp treatment!
  • 5:00 PM: Catch the famous Myeongdong Nanta Show, a hilarious, non-verbal comedy and rhythmic cooking performance.
  • 7:00 PM: The streets transform into the Myeongdong Night Market. Try Tanghulu (candied fruit), viral 10-Won cheese bread, egg bread (Gyeran-ppang), and spicy tteokbokki.

Jeju Island

Fly south for volcanic landscapes, coastal drives, and peak Jeju Cherry Blossoms.

6
Wed, 31 Mar

Hello, Jeju!

  • Morning: Take a short domestic flight from Seoul Gimpo (GMP) to Jeju (CJU). Pick up your rental car at the airport to unlock the island's best hidden gems.
  • 1:00 PM: Drive through Jeonnong-ro Cherry Blossom Street. Late March/Early April is absolute peak bloom in Jeju, and the street forms a breathtaking pink canopy.
  • 3:00 PM: Stop by Yongduam Rock (Dragon Head Rock) near the city center to see the dramatic volcanic coastline, then check into your Jeju accommodation.
  • 6:30 PM: Head to Dongmun Night Market. The vibe is electric! Eat abalone kimbap, torch-grilled pork belly, and try Jeju Galchi (cutlassfish).
7
Thu, 01 Apr

West & South Wonders

  • 9:30 AM: Walk the stunning Aewol Handam Coastal Trail. The clear turquoise water against the black volcanic rock is gorgeous. Stop at a seaside cafe (like Cafe Knotted or Monsant) for coffee.
  • 12:00 PM: Visit the Osulloc Tea Museum. Wander the expansive green tea fields, try a matcha roll cake, and visit the adjacent Innisfree Jeju House.
  • 2:30 PM: Drive by the dramatic Sanbangsan Mountain, beautifully surrounded by oceans of bright yellow canola flowers. Walk down to the Yongmeori Coast (Dragon Head Coast) if the tide allows.
  • 5:00 PM: Head south to Seogwipo to view the spectacular Cheonjiyeon Waterfall, then get an authentic Jeju Black Pork BBQ dinner nearby.
8
Fri, 02 Apr

Sunrise Peak & East Coast

  • 9:00 AM: Hike Seongsan Ilchulbong (Sunrise Peak). This UNESCO World Heritage site offers a short, steep hike culminating in incredible views of a green volcanic crater and the ocean.
  • 1:30 PM: Descend to the nearby cove to watch the famous Haenyeo (Jeju's legendary female free-divers) demonstrate their impressive catch and singing.
  • 3:00 PM: Walk the stunning Seopjikoji coastal trails. This scenic cape is renowned for its romantic lighthouse and rolling hills, heavily featured in Korean dramas.
  • 6:00 PM: Treat yourself to a massive, boiling bowl of Jeju Seafood Ramyeon for dinner, packed with crab, abalone, and mussels.
9
Sat, 03 Apr

Udo Island & Return to Seoul

  • 9:00 AM: Take the short 15-minute passenger ferry from Seongsan Port across to the miniature paradise of Udo Island.
  • 10:30 AM: Rent electric bikes or a small electric car to circumnavigate the island. Stop at the pristine white sand beaches (like Seobin Baeksa) and make sure to try the famous Udo Peanut Ice Cream!
  • 3:00 PM: Ferry back to the mainland, drop off your rental car, and fly from Jeju (CJU) back to Seoul Gimpo (GMP).
  • 7:30 PM: Check back into your Seoul hotel. Keep dinner simple tonight—do as the locals do and order 'Chimaek' (Korean fried chicken and beer) for delivery directly to your hotel room.

Seoul Part 2

Return to the capital just in time for the spectacular cherry blossom peak.

10
Sun, 04 Apr

Hongdae & Cherry Blossoms

  • 10:00 AM: Grab an Isaac Toast for breakfast, then cycle through Yeouido Hangang Park. Walk the famous cherry blossom tunnels lined with over 1,800 blossoming Yoshino cherry trees!
  • 12:30 PM: Step indoors to The Hyundai Seoul, arguably Korea's most beautiful and massive modern shopping mall, complete with an indoor botanical garden.
  • 4:00 PM: Explore the vibrant university district of Hongdae for youthful energy and street fashion. Grab your friends and pack into a "Life4Cuts" or "Photoism" booth for fun, accessorized souvenir printouts!
  • 7:30 PM: Eat some street food while watching the incredible indie buskers and dance troupes on Hongdae Walking Street. Finish the night by renting a Noraebang (karaoke) room!
11
Mon, 05 Apr

Palaces & Culture

  • 9:30 AM: Explore Changdeokgung Palace, focusing on the breathtaking "Secret Garden" (make sure to book this specific tour in advance online!).
  • 12:30 PM: Have lunch at Tosokchon Samgyetang, renowned for serving the best ginseng chicken soup in the city, located in a sprawling traditional hanok.
  • 2:30 PM: Walk off lunch by exploring the hilly, picturesque streets of Samcheong-dong, full of galleries and boutique cafes.
  • 5:30 PM: Take the scenic cable car up to N Seoul Tower. Watch the sunset over the sprawling city, check out the famous "Love Locks" terrace, and grab dinner on the mountain.
12
Tue, 06 Apr

Nami Island Day Trip

  • 8:30 AM: Escape the city for a day trip to Nami Island (via the ITX train to Gapyeong or a pre-booked shuttle tour). Rent a bike to ride through the iconic Metasequoia and Ginkgo tree-lined avenues.
  • 1:00 PM: Grab lunch in the area—you absolutely must try Dakgalbi (spicy stir-fried chicken with cheese and rice cakes), which is the famous local specialty of the Gapyeong region!
  • 3:00 PM: Visit the nearby Garden of Morning Calm. This massive botanical garden is breathtaking in the spring when thousands of flowers and trees begin to bloom.
  • 7:30 PM: Arrive back in Seoul. Farewell Dinner! Treat yourselves to premium, melt-in-your-mouth Korean Hanwoo Beef BBQ (try Maple Tree House or a similar upscale spot).
13
Wed, 07 Apr

Departure

  • 7:30 AM: Do a final sweep of the convenience store for snacks to bring home, then take the AREX train back to Incheon International Airport (ICN). Enjoy some final duty-free shopping.
  • 11:05 AM: Flight SQ 611 departs. Have a safe journey back to Perth!
