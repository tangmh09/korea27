Itinerary Overview

Chasing Cherry Blossoms

13 Days exploring the historical streets of Seoul and the natural volcanic wonders of Jeju Island during peak spring bloom.

Dates

26 Mar - 07 Apr

Destinations

Seoul & Jeju

To-Do List

Don't forget to book your domestic flights from Seoul Gimpo (GMP) to Jeju (CJU) for Mar 31, and the return for Apr 3. Also, secure a rental car in Jeju in advance!

Seoul Part 1

Settle in and explore the vibrant neighborhoods of South Korea's capital.

1
Fri, 26 Mar

Arrival & Welcome

  • 3:40 PM: Arrive at Incheon Airport (ICN). Pick up a T-Money transit card at a convenience store, then catch the AREX Express Train directly to the city center.
  • 5:30 PM: Check into your hotel and freshen up. Let the jetlag wear off!
  • 7:00 PM: Welcome to Korea! Head out to a nearby bustling area (like Myeongdong) to grab your first taste of authentic Korean BBQ and stroll through the neon-lit streets.
2
Sat, 27 Mar

Namdaemun & Gwangjang

  • 10:00 AM: Dive into Namdaemun Market, Korea's oldest and largest traditional market. Hunt for bargains, souvenirs, and make sure to have lunch in the famous Kalguksu (knife-cut noodle) Alley.
  • 2:30 PM: Take the scenic cable car up to N Seoul Tower (very close to Namdaemun). Enjoy panoramic views of the sprawling city and check out the famous "Love Locks" terrace.
  • 7:00 PM: Dinner at the historic Gwangjang Market. Grab a seat at a lively stall and order Bindaetteok (crispy mung bean pancakes), Mayak Gimbap, and homemade dumplings (Netflix famous!).
3
Sun, 28 Mar

Seongsu Area

  • 10:00 AM: Rent a bike or stroll through Seoul Forest Park. It's a massive green oasis featuring vibrant tulip gardens, botanical houses, and early spring blooms.
  • 1:00 PM: Explore Seongsu-dong (often called the "Brooklyn of Seoul"). Check out the flagship Dior concept store and industrial warehouses converted into massive, highly-aesthetic cafes (make sure to visit Cafe Onion or Nudake).
  • 5:30 PM: Visit the futuristic Dongdaemun Design Plaza (DDP), designed by Zaha Hadid. Have dinner nearby and explore the massive wholesale fashion night markets that begin to open as it gets dark.
4
Mon, 29 Mar

Gangnam & Insadong

  • 10:00 AM: Head south of the river to Gangnam. Visit the Starfield COEX Mall and snap a photo in front of the colossal, floor-to-ceiling Starfield Library.
  • 2:30 PM: Take the subway back north to the Insadong district. This area is dedicated to traditional Korean culture. Browse local artisan crafts, galleries, and the spiral Ssamziegil mall.
  • 4:30 PM: Stop by a traditional teahouse (like Shin Old Tea House) in the alleys of Insadong.
  • 6:30 PM: Take a relaxing dusk walk along the beautifully restored Cheonggyecheon Stream before grabbing dinner nearby.
5
Tue, 30 Mar

Myeongdong Area

  • 10:30 AM: Spend the morning exploring the massive Shinsegae or Lotte Duty-Free Department stores for high-end goods and incredible food halls.
  • 2:00 PM: Dive deep into Myeongdong. Visit the Olive Young flagship store to stock up on K-beauty skincare. Consider booking a famous Korean skin-clinic facial!
  • 6:30 PM: The streets transform into the Myeongdong Night Market. Graze on street food like hotteok (sweet pancakes), tteokbokki, Tanghulu (candied fruit), and the viral 10 Won bread.

Jeju Island

Fly south for volcanic landscapes, coastal drives, and peak Jeju Cherry Blossoms.

6
Wed, 31 Mar

Hello, Jeju!

  • Morning: Take a short domestic flight from Seoul Gimpo (GMP) to Jeju (CJU). Pick up your rental car at the airport to unlock the island's best hidden gems.
  • 11:30 AM: Drive through Jeonnong-ro Cherry Blossom Street. Late March/Early April is absolute peak bloom in Jeju, and the street forms a breathtaking pink canopy.
  • 2:00 PM: Walk the Aewol Handam Coastal Trail. The crystal clear water against the black volcanic rock is gorgeous. Stop at a famous seaside cafe (like Cafe Knotted) for a sweet treat.
7
Thu, 01 Apr

West & South Wonders

  • 9:30 AM: Visit the Osulloc Tea Museum. Wander the expansive green tea fields, try a matcha roll cake, and visit the adjacent Innisfree Jeju House to make your own volcanic clay soap.
  • 12:00 PM: Drive by the dramatic Sanbangsan Mountain, which in springtime is famously surrounded by oceans of bright yellow canola flowers.
  • 4:00 PM: Head south to Seogwipo to view the spectacular Cheonjiyeon Waterfall, accessible via a beautifully landscaped walking path.
8
Fri, 02 Apr

Sunrise Peak & East Coast

  • 9:00 AM: Hike Seongsan Ilchulbong (Sunrise Peak). This UNESCO World Heritage site offers a short, steep hike culminating in incredible views of a green volcanic crater and the ocean.
  • 11:30 AM: Descend to the nearby cove to watch the famous Haenyeo (Jeju's legendary female free-divers) demonstrate their impressive catch.
  • 2:30 PM: Walk the stunning Seopjikoji coastal trails. This scenic cape is renowned for its romantic lighthouse and rolling hills, heavily featured in Korean dramas.
9
Sat, 03 Apr

Udo Island & Return to Seoul

  • 9:00 AM: Take the short 15-minute passenger ferry from Seongsan Port across to the miniature paradise of Udo Island.
  • 10:30 AM: Rent electric bikes or a small electric car to circumnavigate the island. Stop at the pristine white sand beaches and make sure to try the famous Udo Peanut Ice Cream!
  • 2:30 PM: Catch the ferry back, then visit Dongmun Traditional Market to buy edible souvenirs like Hallabong orange chocolates.
  • 5:00 PM: Drop off your rental car and fly from Jeju (CJU) back to Seoul Gimpo (GMP). Check into your Seoul hotel and rest.

Seoul Part 2

Return to the capital just in time for the spectacular cherry blossom peak.

10
Sun, 04 Apr

Hongdae & Cherry Blossoms

  • 10:00 AM: Rent bikes and cycle through Yeouido Hangang Park. Walk the famous cherry blossom tunnels lined with over 1,800 blossoming Yoshino cherry trees!
  • 12:30 PM: Have a picnic by the Han River. Do as the locals do and order 'Chimaek' (Korean fried chicken and beer) for delivery right to your picnic mat.
  • 4:00 PM: Explore the vibrant university district of Hongdae for youthful energy, street fashion, incredible indie buskers, and an eclectic nightlife scene. Rent a Noraebang (karaoke) room to finish the night!
11
Mon, 05 Apr

Palaces & Culture

  • 9:30 AM: Explore Gyeongbokgung Palace. Pro-tip: Rent a Hanbok from a nearby shop—it gets you free palace admission and makes for incredible souvenir photos. Catch the Royal Guard Changing Ceremony at 10 AM.
  • 1:00 PM: Have lunch at Tosokchon Samgyetang, renowned for serving the best ginseng chicken soup in the city, located in a traditional hanok.
  • 3:00 PM: Wander the charming, hilly alleys of Bukchon Hanok Village. It's a real residential neighborhood preserved to look as it did in the Joseon Dynasty.
  • 7:00 PM: Head to Itaewon / Hannam-dong for an upscale dining experience, such as premium K-BBQ at Maple Tree House.
12
Tue, 06 Apr

Nami Island Day Trip

  • 8:30 AM: Escape the city for a day trip to Nami Island (via the ITX train to Gapyeong or a booked shuttle tour). Stroll or rent a bike to ride through the iconic Metasequoia and Ginkgo tree-lined avenues.
  • 1:00 PM: Grab lunch in the area—you absolutely must try Dakgalbi (spicy stir-fried chicken), which is the famous local specialty of the Gapyeong region!
  • 3:00 PM: Visit the nearby Garden of Morning Calm. This massive botanical garden is breathtaking in the spring when thousands of flowers and trees begin to bloom.
  • 7:30 PM: Arrive back in Seoul. Farewell Dinner! Enjoy some final K-BBQ or fried chicken to end your trip perfectly.
13
Wed, 07 Apr

Departure

  • 7:30 AM: Take the AREX train back to Incheon International Airport (ICN). Enjoy some final duty-free shopping.
  • 11:05 AM: Flight SQ 611 departs. Have a safe journey back to Perth!
